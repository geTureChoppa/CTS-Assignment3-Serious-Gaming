Chosen Technology/Topic: Digital Simulations and Serious Gaming, Education

Why this topic?

We have chosen this topic as it sparks our interests, particularly the advancements made in digital simulations and gaming. As previously mentioned, extra material in different formats was made accessible to students due to online learning. These materials could come in the form of different useful sources such as RStudio, photoshop etc Relating this to the serious gaming aspect, platforms such as Minecraft, the sims and other entertaining resources have also been deemed as useful in education. It piques our interest as we are firm believers that making education fun is also a convenient way to help students in getting motivation to learn.

What is the level of analysis - culture or organization; It is possible to be viewed from both levels as it involves both culture and organisation.
Culture Level Analysis: At the culture level, the focus would be on how digital simulation and gaming in education are influenced by broader cultural factors. This includes examining cultural attitudes towards technology, the role of technology in education within a specific society or community, and the cultural values and norms that shape the adoption and use of digital simulations and gaming in educational settings. Cultural factors may influence the acceptance, perception, and integration of these technologies in education.
Organization Level Analysis: At the organization level, the focus would be on how educational institutions, such as schools or universities, incorporate and implement digital simulation and gaming in their educational practices. This involves examining the organizational strategies, policies, and practices related to the use of these technologies, as well as the organizational culture and structure that support their integration. Factors such as leadership, resources, training, and decision-making processes within educational organizations would be considered at this level.

Responsibility - individual or pro fessional;Both individual and professional relationships are important in understanding the impact and implications of digital simulation and gaming in education. The individual relationship focuses on the learner's experience and the benefits they derive from engaging with these tools, while the professional relationship examines how educational professionals facilitate and support the use of simulations and gaming for educational purposes.

Ethical issue - power or equity; Power: Digital simulations and gaming can introduce power dynamics within the educational context. Power imbalances can emerge between students, educators, and educational institutions, as well as between developers or providers of educational simulations and games and the end-users. Ethical concerns may arise when power is unequally distributed, leading to issues such as exploitation, manipulation, or the imposition of certain ideologies or values through the use of simulations and gaming.

Technology - medical, nuclear, mobile; Accuracy and Realism: In medical and nuclear simulations, accuracy and realism are crucial for effective learning. It is important to ensure that the simulations and games provide accurate representations of medical procedures, nuclear processes, or mobile technology concepts. Misrepresentations or inaccuracies can lead to false understandings and potentially harm students' knowledge and skills development. Inclusivity and Diversity: Educational simulations and games should strive for inclusivity and diversity. This means representing a wide range of cultures, backgrounds, and perspectives to create an inclusive learning environment. Avoiding stereotypes, biases, or discriminatory content is essential to ensure fair representation and equal opportunities for all learners. Safety and Risk Mitigation: Simulations and games involving medical procedures or nuclear scenarios should prioritize safety. Students should be guided to understand the limitations and potential risks of the simulated activities. Ensuring that the simulations provide a safe environment for learning and that appropriate safety protocols are followed is vital.

ex. Martin, C. D., Huff, C., Gotterbarn, D., & Miller, K. (1996). A framework for implementing and teaching the social and ethical impact of computing. Education and Information Technologies, 1(2), 101-122.
